### Key Trading Terms
<Accordion title="Essential Vocabulary">
  **Bull Market**: A period of rising prices and positive sentiment
  
  **Bear Market**: A period of falling prices and negative sentiment
  
  **Volume**: The total amount of an asset traded in a specific timeframe
  
  **Volatility**: The degree of price variation over time
  
  **Liquidity**: How easily an asset can be bought or sold without affecting price
  
  **Support**: A price level where buying interest is strong enough to prevent further decline
  
  **Resistance**: A price level where selling pressure prevents further price increases
</Accordion>

## üí° Trading Strategies

### Day Trading
**Characteristics:**
- Hold positions for minutes to hours
- Capitalize on short-term price movements
- Requires active monitoring and quick decisions

**Best Practices:**
- Start with small position sizes
- Use stop-losses on every trade
- Focus on high-volume, liquid assets
- Set daily profit/loss limits

### Swing Trading
**Characteristics:**
- Hold positions for days to weeks
- Trade based on technical patterns and trends
- Less time-intensive than day trading

**Key Techniques:**
- Identify support and resistance levels
- Use trend-following strategies
- Look for chart patterns (triangles, flags, etc.)
- Combine technical and fundamental analysis

### Position Trading
**Characteristics:**
- Hold positions for weeks to months
- Based on fundamental analysis and long-term trends
- Requires patience and discipline

**Strategy Elements:**
- Research project fundamentals
- Analyze market cycles
- Dollar-cost averaging
- Portfolio diversification

### Position Sizing
<Accordion title="Risk Per Trade">
  **1% Rule**: Never risk more than 1% of your account on a single trade
  
  **Position Size Formula**: (Account Size √ó Risk %) √∑ (Entry Price - Stop Loss Price)
  
  **Portfolio Allocation**: Limit exposure to any single asset (typically 5-10%)
</Accordion>

### Stop Loss Strategies
<Steps>
  <Step title="Fixed Percentage">
    Set stops at a fixed percentage below entry (e.g., 5%)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Technical Levels">
    Place stops below support levels or above resistance
  </Step>
  <Step title="ATR-Based">
    Use Average True Range to set dynamic stop distances
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trailing Stops">
    Move stops in your favor as the trade becomes profitable
  </Step>
</Steps>
